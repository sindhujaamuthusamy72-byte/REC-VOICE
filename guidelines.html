Posting Guidelines

REC Voice works because people trust it to be fair, factual, and safe to use. These guidelines keep it that way.

✓ Do

  • Describe the issue factually — what happened, where, and how often
  • Refer to people by role or department, e.g. "a lab assistant in the CSE department"
  • Use tags and categories accurately so issues can be tracked properly
  • Upvote ("Me Too") existing issues instead of creating duplicates

✕ Don't

  • Name any individual — staff, student, or otherwise
  • Include identifying details (roll number, room number, specific timings that reveal identity)
  • Post rumors, assumptions, or anything you can't describe factually
  • Use the platform for personal disputes unrelated to college matters

How Issues Get Handled

Status Definitions
Status Meaning
Pending Recently submitted, not yet reviewed by the student council
Under Review Being looked into or discussed with the relevant department
Resolved Action has been taken and the issue is considered closed

A Note on Anonymity

This platform does not collect names, emails, or roll numbers — by design, not by promise. There is nothing to identify you because nothing identifying is ever asked for. That said, please avoid writing details in your description that could indirectly reveal who you are (e.g. "the only student who submitted late on Tuesday").

For urgent safety concerns, please also reach out directly to your college's official grievance redressal cell — this platform is a community tool, not an emergency channel.
